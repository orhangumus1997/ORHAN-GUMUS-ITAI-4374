Key Takeaways from This Course

Biological neurons and artificial neural networks share foundational design principles, but differ in energy efficiency, plasticity, and adaptability.
Hebbian learning ("cells that fire together, wire together") is the biological basis for weight updates in modern neural networks.
Spiking neural networks and neuromorphic computing represent the next frontier in brain-inspired AI.
David Marr's three levels of analysis — computational, algorithmic, and implementational — provide a powerful framework for understanding both brains and AI systems.
The collaboration between neuroscience and AI is not one-directional: each field continues to inform and inspire the other.
